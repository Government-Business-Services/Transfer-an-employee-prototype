How employees move roles across the Civil Service: General principles and further information

This page explains detail behind the staff transfer process.

Eligibility

All civil servants recruited through open and fair competition can apply for, or be considered for a staff transfer on:

  • a level grade
  • a lower grade
  • promotion

Also eligible to apply:

  • employees in crown non-departmental public bodies (NDPBs)
  • employees in accredited non-crown NDPBs (accredited by the Civil Service Commission)
  • employees transferring from the Northern Ireland Civil Service (NICS)

If employees are on a probationary period, the job advert should clarify their eligibility to transfer.

If employees are not sure they are eligible to transfer, they should contact their HR Business Partner.

Employees transferring to the NICS should contact the vacancy holder to check if they will honour their continuous service.

The employee’s transfer

Employees should understand the terms of their transfer in full, including any effect on their:

  • allowances
  • annual leave allowance
  • entitlement to occupational maternity/paternity pay
  • payments into any childcare voucher scheme
  • pension contributions
  • performance award/bonus
  • salary
  • terms and conditions of employment

This list is not exhaustive.

Employees should refer to the job advert or check with their vacancy holder for more details about terms and conditions. Your current department will pay the employee until your last working day with them.

Employees should also check how your performance award/bonus will be paid if this applies. They should speak to you, then their new manager to find out.

Employees will get an employment contract on or before their start date. If they do not, they should contact you. Employees keep employment rights related to length of service. For example, annual leave and pension.

Systems and processes

The current department’s responsibilities

The exporting department is responsible for:

  • following the process and providing accurate information to the employee’s new department with one calendar month notice. This allows for their transfer with minimal disruption to the employee and department.
  • releasing the employee to their new role within 4-8 weeks. Excluding time taken to complete any vetting needed.
  • paying the employee until the day before they transfer.

If their transfer date is the first of the month, this should avoid any overpayments. These include non-recoverable aspects, for example increased:

  • student loan payments
  • pension payments
  • National Insurance payments

The new department’s responsibilities

The new department is responsible for:

  • completing the employee's pre-employment checks
  • transfer of any valid security clearance
    • li>completing a new security clearance request if applicable
  • making sure the employee has a staff number and start date
  • setting up the employee's HR record and paying them from their start date
    • keeping in contact with the employee

Pay and allowances

The new department makes sure:

  • pay
  • allowances (if they apply)
  • information on the terms of the post

are clear in job adverts, agreed and recorded on time so the HR record can be set up.

If the employee is:

  • transferring permanently
  • on a loan of more than 6 months

their new department’s pay and reward policies will apply.

The current department will pay an employee’s salary until the date of transfer. If the employee’s first day in the new department is a Monday then their last day of service will be a Sunday. The new department will pay the employee from their first working day in their new role.

Individual departments are responsible for pay decisions. The employee should check their department’s intranet for further guidance.

Level transfers

Basic pay in level transfers usually stays the same. It excludes, for example:

  • specialist pay or allowances
  • location allowances
  • unsocial hours payments

This list is not exhaustive.

Example 1

The employee’s current basic pay is below the minimum of the new department’s pay range. Their new department will put them on the minimum basic pay for their grade.

Example 2

The employee’s basic pay is above the maximum of the new department’s pay range after level transfer. Their new department will put them on their maximum basic pay for their grade.

The new department's mark time pay policy will decide if the amount above the maximum pay range will be:

  • lost
  • paid on a mark time basis

Promotion

If an employee transfers on promotion, the new department will work out the basic pay for their new grade.

Existing civil servants who gain promotion may:

  • move to the bottom of the new grade pay scale
  • receive a per cent increase in basic pay whichever would be greater

The new department will confirm the basic pay per cent increase.

Example

You are transferring on promotion. Your current pay is below the maximum basic pay for the grade. You may:

  • move to the bottom of the new grade pay scale
  • receive a per cent increase in basic pay (usually 10%)

whichever would be more.

Example 2

An employee transfers to a new department on promotion. Their basic pay is above the top of the new department’s pay range. The new department will place the employee at the top of the basic pay for their grade. The new department's mark time policy will decide if the amount above the top of the pay range will be:

  • lost
  • paid on a mark time basis

Allowances

Allowances are not automatically kept when employees transfer or move on loan to a new department. They are not consolidated into base pay. The new department decides on payable allowances following transfer. Payment of an allowance may include:

  • location
  • specialist professions or skills
  • unsocial hours
  • recruitment and retention

The new department will make sure job adverts state relevant allowances for the role.

If the role does not have a specialist pay range or allowance

The employee will lose any existing specialist pay or allowances. The department’s pay, allowance and mark time policies could have an effect on these. If employees are not sure, they should speak to their new line manager.

Location

Departments have different location pay areas. Employees may not receive the same location pay or allowance, even if their location stays the same.

Performance awards

End of year performance award

Entitlement depends on the transfer date to the employee’s new department.

Example 1: An employee transfers after the old department’s settlement date

The old department will honour the payment. Departments keep payroll open for 6 months after transfers. This is so they can process any outstanding payments. Employees should not receive two performance awards for the same performance period.

Example 2: An employee transfers before both department’s settlement dates. This is after the end of the performance year

Their new department will pay their award on their settlement date if appropriate.

If it is not possible or practical for the old department to pay an employee’s performance award:

  • the expectation is that the new department will honour the payment

National Security Vetting (NSV)

If the employee holds a National Security Vetting (NSV) clearance level or they need one for their new role, this is part of your transfer process.

NSV is sometimes called security clearance. It sits with the employee, not their role.

If they have a valid NSV, it will be transferable if the expiry date is longer than 3 months, unless there is:

  • a risk flag
  • an issue with the transfer

The employee will be told if the NSV transfer will take more than 7 days. For example:

  • active after care is present
  • NSV is not found

The employee should always complete part A-1 of the staff transfer form. They should only complete the NSV section if:

  • their new role needs NSV - regardless of current clearance
  • they have valid NSV clearance - regardless of level, even if their new role does not need NSV clearance

Pre-employment checks

The employee's new role may need them to have pre-employment checks. This could include checks on:

  • Baseline Personnel Security Standard (BPSS)
  • relationships with current prisoners
  • safeguarding checks to work with children or vulnerable adults

This list is not exhaustive.
